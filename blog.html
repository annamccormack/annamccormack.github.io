Emotional Intelligence

Sprint Three Cultural Blog | Tuesday 24th November, 2020

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence (EI) or Emotional Quotient (EQ) is being aware of your own emotions and being able to identify and manage them. It is also about being aware of how those emotions affect those around you along with an awareness for how others are feeling.

The four attributes of emotional intelligence are:

  • Self-management
  • Self-awareness
  • Social awareness
  • Relationship management

How is it different to IQ?

Intelligence quotient (IQ) is a number to express a person's intelligence relative to their age. It is a measure of a personâ€™s reasoning ability and how they use logic to answer a question or solve a problem. IQ can also be used to define an individual's knowledge and proficiency of a specific topic.

Why is emotional intelligence important?

EQ is important as it allows you to communicate effectively and work productively with others. It also also allows you to adapt to change and challenges when they arise more easily.

The difference between Margin, Border, and Padding

Sprint Two Technical Blog | Sunday 22nd November, 2020

The best way to describe the difference between these three properties is to use the box model. In CSS the box model consists of layers that wrap around every HTML element. These layers are: border, padding, margin, and the element itself.

Margin

Specifies the amount of space between the border and the outside edge of the element. Each side of the margin layer can also be specified individually.

Border

Specifies the amount of space between the padding of the content area and the margin. This property specifies the width of the border along with the border style and colour.

Padding

Specifies the amount of space between the content area and the border. Each side of the padding layer can also be specified individually.

Content

The area of the HTML element itself. The size of the content is specified by width and height.

Identity, Values and Strengths Exploration

Sprint One Cultural Blog | Thursday 12th November, 2020

Describe your core personal values.

This year has been a year of reflection for me with my plans having to change so much. I have used this year to take a step back and reflect on my personal values and my goals for the future. I would summarize my core personal values as integrity, balance, kindness, purpose, and growth.

  • Integrity is an important part of my identity as I value honesty and staying true to myself.
  • It is important to me to treat everyone with kindness and compassion even when I do not agree, I still find it important to try and understand their situation.
  • I am always striving to create balance in my life between approaching life with structure vs. being flexible and adapting to changes.
  • I highly value having a purpose and making a difference in my own way. I believe that everyone can make a difference in their lifetime no matter how small or big the effect may be.
  • I value growth, professionally and personally. I have found I get the most joy and engagement out of a job when I can continue to learn and I am being challenged to improve myself and my outcomes.

Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

Having worked in the fashion industry which is known as one of the most polluting industries in the world along with a lot of questionable ethical practices I have spent a lot of time researching these issues. With the knowledge of how much of an impact unsustainable and unethical fashion has made my decisions when consuming fashion an ethical one. One of the big ethical decisions is the choice between the use of leather vs. faux leather shoes.

With leather, the pros are that it is a long-lasting and hard-wearing product that will last a long time plus it is a by-product of the meat industry and will eventually decompose. The cons are that most leather is tanned using high levels of toxic chemicals, there is potential animal cruelty in the supply chain and the leather trade is contributing to the deforestation of theamazon.

But then we look at the alternative which is faux leather which is a plastic-based synthetic material. The pros are that there is no animal cruelty involved in the supply chain, and it is durable. The cons are that it is another plastic product which will not decompose, and also contains harmful chemicals.

So how do I decide between real and faux leather after knowing all the facts? My decision is only to buy leather if I am sourcing my shoes from a company who is doing their best to minimise their impact. For example, some companies are choosing to use vegetable tanning, who are ensuring that they do not contribute to deforestation and are ethically sourced. So when I do decide to buy a leather product I do my research and I make sure that I care for my shoes so they last for as long as possible.

Describe your strengths and limitations.

My strengths are that I am a hard worker, I am flexible, I bring a creative mindset to my work, I am a team player and I am always open to new experiences and learning new skills. My limitations are that I am quite self-critical and a bit of a perfectionist which can prevent me from moving on from a task or result in spending too much time on the little details. I also need to work having confidence in my ideas and communicating my ideas and work to others effectively.

Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development.

My flexibility, my willingness to give any task a try or help anyone else out has been an asset to my previous career in fashion and will be something I bring into my new career path. I have found that my desire to be learning new skills and having variety in my work has resulted in outgrowing my roles but I have been held back being self-critical and having a lack of opportunities in the industry. Being very self-critical and trying to perfect my work has gotten in the way of my learning in the past.

Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles.

I believe all my strengths will aid me in succeeding in my learning journey at EDA. I can use my flexibility, perseverance and creative mindset to overcome obstacles and problem solve. Being a team player will be helpful while completing group tasks. The obstacles I will encounter will be self-doubt and self-criticism which might tell me my work isn't good enough or I won't be smart enough to complete the bootcamp and become a web developer.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

I was working in a team and we had a deadline to meet. Each team member had tasks to complete along the chain. I had completed as much work as I could and was awaiting more work from a colleague who had a lot of work to do and was overwhelmed. I offered to help in any way I could, however, they declined. This resistance my colleague had to give up control of their work by allowing me to help was frustrating as I could have easily helped to lighten my colleagues' workload and ultimately meet our deadline sooner. The way I dealt with this situation was by waiting to approach my colleague when they were not in a position of stress. I then asked to be shown how they complete their tasks. This meant the next time we needed to meet a deadline I was able to offer my colleague help, my colleague felt comfortable in sharing their work, and we were more productive as a team.
