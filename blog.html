The difference between Margin, Border, and Padding

Sprint Two Technical Blog | Sunday 22nd November, 2020

The best way to describe the difference between these three properties is to use the box model. In CSS the box model consists of layers that wrap around every HTML element. These layers are: border, padding, margin, and the element itself.

Margin

Specifies the amount of space between the border and the outside edge of the element. Each side of the margin layer can also be specified individually.

Border

Specifies the amount of space between the padding of the content area and the margin. This property specifies the width of the border along with the border style and colour.

Padding

Specifies the amount of space between the content area and the border. Each side of the padding layer can also be specified individually.

Content

The area of the HTML element itself. The size of the content is specified by width and height.

Learning Plan

Sprint Two Technical Blog | Sunday 22nd November, 2020

What is your long term goal and or career pathway?

My short term goal is to successfully navigate the EDA boot camp and be in a position to apply for Junior Web Development roles. My long term goal would be to use the skills and experience I gain in New Zealand to eventually be able to move to Amsterdam. It is important to me that my career pathway provides me with the opportunity for growth and gives me a purpose where I can make a difference.


What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

An area I would like to work on while at EDA is my confidence when communicating or presenting my ideas to others. This is something I found hard while studying Fashion Design and an area I wish to continue to improve in so I can confidently communicate my ideas successfully.


A description of your own strengths and limitations.

I am someone that values integrity, kindness, balance, purpose and growth. My strengths are that I am a hard worker, I am flexible, I bring a creative mindset to my work, and I am ateam player. My limitations are that I am quite self-critical and a bit of a perfectionist which can stop me from progressing a task as I can spend too much time on the little details.


Based on the above description, a commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

I will set clear boundaries between when I am studying and my free time. This will ensure I will not spend too much time perfecting little things and instead use time-boxing to focus on the bigger picture. It will be important for me to practice speaking up about my ideas and work on communicating those to other learners who I may be collaborating with along with the facilitators.


A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

When I require help, after I have tried debugging and searching the internet I will ensure I either ask my peers via slack or asking my facilitator directly.


A description of what you expect from the facilitation team.

I expect an open and honest relationship with the facilitators so I can feel fully comfortable to come to them for help and technical support.


Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things.

During Foundations, I will be working two days per week, so I need to plan my week wisely to ensure I complete my work while also having a balance of time not studying or working. I plan to block out ten minutes each day to plan and review my learning, even on the days I am working, just to check in and keep on track with each sprint.
