Problem Solving

Sprint Four Technical Blog | Saturday 5th December, 2020

During the Built-in Methods kata, I found I got stuck on the deBee function. With some googling, and trying the rubber ducky method by talking to my fern plant, I figured how to combine all the in-built methods correctly. Yet, the function was still not passing the tests! Why?! I had simply forgotten to include the brackets ( ) at the end of the .toLowerCase() method. This challenge taught me the importance of checking for spelling and syntax mistakes and using the error messages to help me solve the problem.

During the kata challenges this week, I felt like I was able to solve the JS Olympics challenges quite elegantly. I was on a roll from all the previous kata challenges and felt quite confident in my problem-solving abilities after successfully working through all the previous problems. I learnt that using pseudocode is a helpful tool for planning my solution before writing any code.

Reflecting on my confidence in using problem-solving techniques and processes.

Pseudocode

Is not actual code, but is non-technical steps, written plainly that create the rough draft of your code. I have found pseudocode a very important step in my problem-solving process, and one of the first steps I take when planning my code. It allows me to break down the large challenge into smaller manageable tasks and to have a greater understanding of what my code will do.

Trying something

By simply attempting something you have started to solve the problem because if it works then great and if it doesn't then it is a starting point from where you can apply other problem-solving techniques. By giving the code a try you will get error messages that may help you figure out what you have done wrong and may lead to questions that can be answered by researching further.

Reading error messages

When an error occurs, JavaScript will normally stop, and generate an error message. Reading these error messages can be a very useful way to debug your code. The error message provides the location/ line of the error and the type of error. Some examples of errors messages include Syntax errors, Expected or unexpected parenthesis and brackets, X Is Not defined: when a variable has not been defined. I have found reading through error messages great at pinpointing why my code is not working.

The Rubber Ducky Method

Is a method of debugging code. It involves explaining the problem to an inanimate object like a rubber duck. Typically in the process of doing this, you’ll find your problem. I don't have a rubber duck but I have tried discussing my problems out loud to my houseplants. I do feel a little silly and have had mixed results from this technique.

Console.logging

console.log() is a function that writes a message to log on the console. This is a useful way to test sections of code to see if they are functioning.

Googling

Google is a great tool as it can provide you with the knowledge you need. Any problem you have when coding, someone else has had before or has the answer. Keeping to concise and simple questions when googling can find these answers quickly. I found myself using google a lot during the kata challenges, for finding out about methods or getting past a problem, googling has been a very helpful tool.

Asking your peers and coaches for help

I haven't had any many instances during the foundations of needing to ask for help from peers or coaches but when I do need that help I am comfortable to do so.

Reflection

"We do not learn from experience ... we learn from reflecting on experience." | John Dewey

I have found using reflection as a great way to improve my process. Reflecting at the end of a task means I understand what I have learnt and ensures I have met the learning competencies and identifies areas I may need to work on. I have gotten into the habit of starting each day of study with these four questions:

  • What are my goals today?
  • What are my challenges today?
  • Have I reached my goals for today?
  • What have I learnt today?

Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Sprint Four Cultural Blog | Tuesday 1st December, 2020

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity refers to the brain’s ability to adapt to new situations and is the process by which all permanent learning takes place in your brain. Every time we learn something new, we are harnessing the power of neuroplasticity. When you repeatedly practise an activity or access a memory the neural pathways of the brain are changed, these connections are constantly becoming stronger or weaker according to our experiences. Repeated thinking, feeling and doing strengthens a pathway and makes it easier to follow this pathway. When we stop practising, the brain will eventually eliminate the cells that formed the pathways. Its like any other muscle in our body, if you don't use it, you lose it.

Understanding how neuroplasticity works gives us the knowledge that we can learn and change our way of thinking and that our abilities are not fixed and can be improved on through repetition.

How to improve neuroplasticity:
  • Quality sleep.
  • Learning something new.
  • Regular exercise.
  • Reduce stress through mindfulness.
  • Find a purpose for why you are learning
  • Read a novel.

Growth Mindset

The growth mindset is the understanding that your abilities can be developed through sustained effort. Whereas, a fixed mindset is the belief that your intelligence and abilities are fixed. So when conflicts arise a fixed mindset usually results in avoidance due to the fear of failure. When you embrace the growth mindset, problems can be seen as opportunities to learn and constructive criticism can be seen as an opportunity to improve. This mindset uses effort and difficulty to create growth and puts importance on trying instead of the final outcome. It is also important to note that we may have a growth mindset in one area and a fixed mindset in another.

A few ways to embrace the growth mindset are:
  • View challenges as opportunities.
  • Replace failure with learning.
  • Value the process over the final outcome.
  • Utilise the knowledge of neuroplasticity and practice practice practice.

Sprint Three Technical Blog | Sunday 29th November, 2020

An analogy to describe the differences between HTML and CSS.

HTML defines the content and structure of a webpage. HTML would be the skeleton of a body. The skeleton holds everything together and provides the base to support organs and muscles. HTML creates all the content of a website however it doesn't do very much on its own and it won’t look very nice. This is why CSS is required to style the HTML elements. If HTML is the skeleton, then CSS is the skin. CSS changes the appearance of websites, by defining colour, fonts, layouts etc. The styling is what makes a web page more appealing to look at.


Explain control flow and loops using an example process from everyday life.

Control flow is the order in which statements and functions are executed, which is from top to bottom, in the order that they appear. This flow can be broken up by conditional statements and loops.

Loops are a way to repeatedly run some code. A condition is required to determine how long the loop will iterate.

Looping statements in JavaScript:
  • For: Allows us to repeatedly run some code until an expression we specify returns false.
  • While: Repeats some code until its condition returns false.
  • Do… While: has its conditional expression at the end, this ensures that the code inside the loop will run at least once.

An example of control flow and loops in everyday life would be when following a recipe for a banana cake.

  1. Cream the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  2. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition
  3. Add the mashed banana and mix well.
  4. Stir the baking soda into the hot milk and add to the creamed mixture.
  5. Sift the dry ingredients together and fold through the creamed mixture.
  6. Scoop the mixture into a tin, and bake at 180ºC for 50 minutes or until the cake springs back when lightly touched.
  7. Leave in tin for 10 minutes before turning out onto a wire rack to cool. When cold, ice with Chocolate Icing or dust with icing sugar.

This recipe is an example of control flow where you must complete each task from top to bottom. For each step there are tasks that are repeated until a condition has been met.

The DOM (Document Object Model) is a hierarchical structure that the browser uses to make sense of a document. Everything that makes up the DOM are known as nodes. The nodes of every document are organized in a tree structure. It is the way through which Javascript can access and modify elements of a HTML document.

Explain what functions are and why they are useful.

A function is a set of reusable statements which perform a particular task.

A function declaration consists of the function keyword, the name of the function, a list of parameters in parentheses and separated by commas, and enclosed in curly brackets the function statements. Defining the function doesn't execute the function it only names the function and specifies what to do when the function is called. Calling the function is what actually invokes the specified action and includes parameters.

Functions are important as it enables you to quickly and easily reuse code. Once the function is defined, it can be called many times including with different arguments (parameters) to produce different results.

Emotional Intelligence

Sprint Three Cultural Blog | Tuesday 24th November, 2020

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence (EI) or Emotional Quotient (EQ) is being aware of your own emotions and being able to identify and manage them. It is also about being aware of how those emotions affect those around you along with an awareness for how others are feeling.

The four attributes of emotional intelligence are:

  • Self-management
  • Self-awareness
  • Social awareness
  • Relationship management

How is it different to IQ?

Intelligence quotient (IQ) is a number to express a person's intelligence relative to their age. It is a measure of a person’s reasoning ability and how they use logic to answer a question or solve a problem. IQ can also be used to define an individual's knowledge and proficiency of a specific topic.

Why is emotional intelligence important?

EQ is important as it allows you to communicate effectively and work productively with others. It also also allows you to adapt to change and challenges when they arise more easily.

The difference between Margin, Border, and Padding

Sprint Two Technical Blog | Sunday 22nd November, 2020

The best way to describe the difference between these three properties is to use the box model. In CSS the box model consists of layers that wrap around every HTML element. These layers are: border, padding, margin, and the element itself.

Margin

Specifies the amount of space between the border and the outside edge of the element. Each side of the margin layer can also be specified individually.

Border

Specifies the amount of space between the padding of the content area and the margin. This property specifies the width of the border along with the border style and colour.

Padding

Specifies the amount of space between the content area and the border. Each side of the padding layer can also be specified individually.

Content

The area of the HTML element itself. The size of the content is specified by width and height.

Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

Having worked in the fashion industry which is known as one of the most polluting industries in the world along with a lot of questionable ethical practices I have spent a lot of time researching these issues. With the knowledge of how much of an impact unsustainable and unethical fashion has made my decisions when consuming fashion an ethical one. One of the big ethical decisions is the choice between the use of leather vs. faux leather shoes.

With leather, the pros are that it is a long-lasting and hard-wearing product that will last a long time plus it is a by-product of the meat industry and will eventually decompose. The cons are that most leather is tanned using high levels of toxic chemicals, there is potential animal cruelty in the supply chain and the leather trade is contributing to the deforestation of theamazon.

But then we look at the alternative which is faux leather which is a plastic-based synthetic material. The pros are that there is no animal cruelty involved in the supply chain, and it is durable. The cons are that it is another plastic product which will not decompose, and also contains harmful chemicals.

So how do I decide between real and faux leather after knowing all the facts? My decision is only to buy leather if I am sourcing my shoes from a company who is doing their best to minimise their impact. For example, some companies are choosing to use vegetable tanning, who are ensuring that they do not contribute to deforestation and are ethically sourced. So when I do decide to buy a leather product I do my research and I make sure that I care for my shoes so they last for as long as possible.

I was working in a team and we had a deadline to meet. Each team member had tasks to complete along the chain.
