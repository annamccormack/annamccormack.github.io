Reflecting on foundations & Preparing for Bootcamp

Sprint Five Cultural Blog | Thursday 10 December, 2020

Reflecting on foundations

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?
  • I have learnt that I had been stuck in a fixed mindset when it came to learning during my previous period of study but when it comes to learning about Web Development I have fully embraced the growth mindset and open to failing in order to grow and learn.
  • I have learnt to reflect on my values and understand what is important to me and what drives me to learn.
  • I have learnt that I can communicate my ideas effectively, I just need to spend some time considering what I am going to say before I speak.

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

I think being self-aware of your values, strengths and limitations makes you a better learner. Self-awareness, empathy and understanding your values are all important aspects of your Emotional Intelligence. If you can understand and manage your feelings you can learn better and if you can empathise with others you can work with others more effectively.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I think what surprised me the most was that what I learnt from the core learning actually resulted in being a better learner.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I found having to summarize myself and my ideas in the blog posts the most challenging aspect of the core learning.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

Core learning has taught me that EQ is just as important as IQ. So by spending time learning about these core subjects, we as students improve our EQ. Increased EQ means we are better at working with others, are more employable and better programmers.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead? Justify your answer.

Spending time on core leaning has not been a waste of time. In some ways, I have found the core work harder than the technical as It has made me reflect on myself and summarise this for each cultural blog. I think my understanding of things like EQ, problem-solving techniques, neuroplasticity and the growth mindset has improved how I learn and problem solve.


Preparing for Bootcamp

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My short term goal is to finish the EDA Bootcamp and I am hoping to be able to use the skills I gain at EDA to successfully get a Junior Web Development role. I think I would aim for a front-end focussed role since I am coming from a design background.

My long term goals include: Finding opportunities to combine web development with my experience in the fashion industry, travelling and be able to work in web development while overseas. A big part of choosing web development is that I value personal growth and having a purpose, and making a difference. So I hope to work in an organisation that would be making some kind of difference.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My strengths of flexibility and being hardworking, and a team player will definitely aid me in my learning in bootcamp. Being self-critical and a bit of a perfectionist will continue to be a limitation that I need to work past. I can be a little reserved when I first meet people, so It will be important that I don't feel too shy to ask for help or contribute to team projects early on in bootcamp.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Speaking or presenting my ideas in front of a group. Also staying on top of all the work and maintaining momentum over the whole nine weeks.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I would like to strengthen my communication skills, along with my confidence in presenting my ideas. I would also like to improve my active listening skills.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

My expectation for myself is to attempt to bring a growth mindset to every challenge I face and to remind myself of taking the just in time approach. I commit to making a plan for each week of bootcamp so that I can manage and keep on top of the workload. This plan will include time for reflection, mindfulness, exercise and relaxation so that I can have some balance between studying and self care.
